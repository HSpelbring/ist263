About Me

I grew up in Richmond, Virginia, where I developed a deep passion for technology, problem-solving, and competition. I attended the Collegiate School, where I spent much of my time balancing academics with playing soccer, a sport I dedicated many years to and that shaped my teamwork and discipline.

Drawn to Syracuse University for its strong computer science program and opportunities to pave my own path, I pursued a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science. Throughout my time at Syracuse, I worked on a variety of technical projects, took on leadership roles, and built meaningful connections. I was an active member of Phi Kappa Theta Fraternity, where I served as Vice President of New Member Development, organizing events and mentoring incoming members. Additionally, I worked as a PepsiCo brand ambassador, engaging with the student community to promote the brand and its products.

Over the summers, I gained valuable industry experience through two internships at Probity Inc., where I worked on cybersecurity initiatives, as well as data and policy analysis.
